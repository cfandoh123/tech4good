01 — The Why

Same skills. Different futures.

The pull toward cybercrime is rarely about talent or curiosity — it is about access. Many young Ghanaians with real technical ability are never shown what an honest, ambitious career in technology actually looks like, or how to get there.

Tech4Good is a summer service initiative founded by Calvin Andoh, a Princeton computer engineering student raised in Nungua, who knows the pressures firsthand. The program runs in two parts: a two-week Tech Advocacy phase that visits five public high schools in greater Accra, and a three-week Innovators Bootcamp that selects 25 outstanding students for intensive technical training.

The curriculum mirrors real engineering team rhythms — daily standups, code reviews, sprint demos. Students leave with a working product, a GitHub portfolio, a pitch they can deliver, and a network that extends from Nungua to Accra to Princeton.

Through partnerships with Bismuth Technologies and the Young Achievers Foundation, the top students leave with paid internships and contract-based opportunities — replacing the cycle of fraud with the beginning of a career.

I want to help break a cycle by giving others the guidance and ethical direction that many of my peers never had.
— Calvin Andoh, Founder
02 — The Program

Built in two movements.

The first reaches breadth — inspiring hundreds of high school students with a vision of what ethical tech careers can look like. The second goes deep — turning the most motivated 25 into builders.

Phase One
Tech Advocacy Program
2 Weeks · 5 Public High Schools · ~500 Students

Classroom visits to introduce students to ethical careers in software, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity. Founder-led storytelling, live build demos, and panels with young Ghanaian engineers.

  • Personal narrative sessions: temptation, choice, trajectory
  • 20-minute live builds (chatbots, image classifiers, translators)
  • "Same Skills, Two Futures" career mapping module
  • Panels with engineers from Bismuth, MEST, Turntabl
  • Transparent audition for bootcamp seats
Phase Two
Innovators Bootcamp
3 Weeks · 25 Students · Full-time

Intensive technical training mirroring real engineering teams. Students learn modern stacks, ship working products, and pitch to a panel of investors and technology leaders on Demo Day.

  • Python, FastAPI, Git/GitHub from day one
  • Daily standups, code reviews, Friday demos
  • Cyber-ethics & defensive security track
  • Remote guest sessions with engineers from Google & beyond
  • Team builds solving real Nungua & Accra problems
  • Final Demo Day pitch to investor panel
